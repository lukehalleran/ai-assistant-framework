feat: Retrieval-based synthesis pipeline, bridge quality cleanup, coherence calibration

Replaces random-pairing synthesis with retrieval-based discovery (Option A)
and graph feedback loop (Option B). After calibrating the coherence judge,
random pairing produced 0/45 accepted candidates. Retrieval-based pipeline
produces 4/30 (13%) with named structural mechanisms.

Retrieval-Based Generator (knowledge/synthesis_retriever.py):
- RetrievalSynthesisGenerator: same interface as SynthesisGenerator (drop-in)
- Few-shot structural query extraction from personal facts (LLM, ~100 tokens)
  with diversity-constrained examples to prevent "feedback loops" convergence
- FAISS semantic search (40M vectors) with similarity floor (0.25)
- Domain filtering: exclude same-domain retrievals
- Adversarial evaluation prompt: "find reasons this does NOT hold"
- Candidates tagged via walk_path=["retrieval", concept_a, concept_b]

Bridge Quality Cleanup:
- GraphMemory.prune_garbage_bridges(): 5-category noise removal
  (mentioned_alongside, low-confidence embedding, misrouted personal,
  acronym collisions, unstructured predicates) — removed 129 garbage edges
- WikidataEntityMapper: embedding threshold 0.60→0.80, exact-match blocklist
  for common-word collisions (cats→CATS, ice→ICE, etc.)
- 6 legitimate bridges remain (Cabo, Eugene, NYC geographic matches)

GraphWalkGenerator Improvements:
- Hub dampening: log-scale penalty for degree > 15 (GRAPH_WALK_HUB_DEGREE_THRESHOLD)
- Cross-domain walk constraint: walks must touch ≥2 domain categories
- Richer source_domains from all walk nodes, not just endpoints

Coherence Judge Recalibration:
- WEAK = no mechanism named, vague bridging language
- MODERATE = names real mechanism applied concretely to both domains
  (generality is not a flaw if application is concrete)
- max_tokens 250→400 (was truncating before Rating line)
- New generic bridge templates: "serve as microcosm", "through the lens of"

Namespace Resolution (SynthesisGenerator):
